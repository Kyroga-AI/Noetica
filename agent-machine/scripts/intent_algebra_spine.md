# The Interpretive Spine — the 23×6 modeled after the structure of the universe

**Status:** SETTLED design directive. This is the decided premise of the system, not a hypothesis to be relitigated. Build it well; do not reopen whether it is "allowed."

The system is modeled after the deepest invariant we know: the duality of **substance and operation** — the thing that *contains* and the thing that *is*. It recurs across every serious account of reality, which is why we build on it:

| tradition | the container (substance) | the form (operation) |
|---|---|---|
| Jewish / Aristotelian hylomorphism | **חומר · chomer** — matter, potency, the receiver | **צורה · tzurah** — form, actuality, the essence |
| grammar | noun · thing | verb · action |
| physics *(metaphor only — see guardrail)* | matter (occupies) | force (interacts) |

## How it maps onto the 23 × 6

- **Substrates = chomer (matter).** `store`, `held`, `world` are the three things that *contain and receive* — persistent matter, internal matter, external matter.
- **Actions = tzurah (form).** The verbs are what gives the matter determinate form. An action *actualizes* a substrate.
- **Read/Write polarity = the receive↔form involution.** `read` is receiving-as (matter-side); `write` is forming-as (form-side). The ℤ/2 polarity flip that closes each substrate **is** the chomer↔tzurah involution — and it is an *adjunction*, not an inverse: form does not undo matter, it reads what matter holds and writes what gives it shape.
- **The +1 meta-row = the containing matter itself.** "The space that contains and relates the other 22; identity / no-op lives here — describe without transforming." That is **pure chomer**: substance prior to form, holding without actualizing. It is not just a row; it is the matter that holds all the forms.

So the grid is not an arbitrary table. **Rows are matter (the 22 domains + the containing meta-row); columns are form (the 6 actions); the cells are matter taking form.** That is the universe's own joint, instantiated as an operating structure.
